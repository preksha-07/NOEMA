Abstract

Traditional manufacturing systems frequently rely on predefined execution paths that are not globally optimized. This often results in excessive machine movement, increased execution time, unnecessary operational cost, higher wear accumulation, and reduced manufacturing efficiency.

NOEMA introduces an intelligent manufacturing framework capable of generating optimized machine trajectories through a combination of image processing, graph-based optimization, Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO), simulated annealing, convergence-driven candidate reduction, and digital twin validation.

The proposed framework transforms geometric designs into manufacturable toolpaths, evaluates thousands of candidate solutions, predicts execution performance before deployment, and continuously improves decision quality through adaptive feedback mechanisms.

Theoretical Foundations

Manufacturing Optimization Theory

Manufacturing path planning belongs to a class of combinatorial optimization problems where the number of possible solutions increases exponentially with the number of execution points.

Traveling Salesman Analogy

Toolpath planning can be represented similarly to the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP), where the objective is to determine the most efficient traversal sequence through a set of locations.

Graph Theory

Geometry points are modeled as graph nodes while potential movements between points are modeled as weighted edges. Optimization is performed over this graph representation.

Stochastic Optimization

Instead of assuming a deterministic search strategy, NOEMA employs probabilistic exploration mechanisms capable of escaping local optima and discovering higher quality solutions.

Probability & Uncertainty

Real manufacturing environments are affected by vibration, positioning errors, mechanical deviations, and environmental disturbances. These uncertainties motivate the incorporation of risk-aware optimization.

Industry 4.0 Principles

NOEMA aligns with Industry 4.0 through the integration of cyber-physical systems, digital twins, predictive analytics, and intelligent manufacturing execution.

Problem Formulation

Toolpath Representation

Extracted contour points are transformed into a graph representation where each point acts as a node and every traversal possibility contributes to the search space explored by the optimization engine.

QUBO Formulation

min xᵀQx

Candidate manufacturing paths are evaluated using a Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization framework, enabling energy-based solution ranking.

Energy Landscape

Every candidate path corresponds to an energy state. Lower energy represents a more desirable manufacturing solution with reduced operational cost.

Simulated Annealing Acceptance

P = e-ΔE/T

Worse solutions may be temporarily accepted with a probability dependent on temperature, enabling global exploration and avoidance of local minima.

Optimization Engine

The optimization engine evaluates a large number of candidate manufacturing paths and identifies the most efficient solution through cost-aware, risk-aware and energy-based optimization.

Candidate Generation

Multiple candidate solutions are generated through path exploration mechanisms. These candidates form the search space explored by the optimization engine.

Path Evaluation

Every candidate is evaluated using manufacturing metrics including distance, risk and execution cost.

Cost Function

Cost =wd·Distance + wt·Time + wr·Risk + wp·TurnPenalty + ws·SmoothnessPenalty + wv·SpeedPenalty + wj·JumpPenalty
Current implementation evaluates manufacturing efficiency using a weighted cost model.

where, wd = distance weight, wt = time weight, wr = risk weight, wp = turn weight, ws = smoothness weight, wv = speed weight, wj = jump weight

The architecture supports future integration of execution time, risk and constraint penalties.
